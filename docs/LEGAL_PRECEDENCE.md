## 3. Legal Framework

### 3.1 Blockchain Timestamps as Legal Evidence

**Marseille Judicial Court — March 2025**

In a landmark ruling, the Marseille Judicial Court became the **first court to recognize blockchain timestamps as legitimate evidence** of prior existence and ownership in copyright disputes (AZ Factory fashion design case).

**Relevance to ORION:**
- ORION's 484+ SHA-256 proofs function identically to blockchain timestamps
- Each proof contains: timestamp, event type, payload, owner identification, and ORION_ID
- Sequential proof chain creates immutable chronological evidence
- This format is now court-admissible under French/EU jurisprudence

### 3.2 EU AI Act (August 2025)

The EU AI Act enforcement began August 2025, establishing:
- Transparency requirements for AI-generated content
- Mandatory documentation of AI system provenance
- Auditability requirements for AI development processes
- Fines up to **7% of global revenue** for non-compliance

**Relevance to ORION:**
- ORION's proof chain constitutes exactly the type of provenance documentation the EU AI Act mandates
- Any derivative system (e.g., Agent V3) must demonstrate independent provenance — or face regulatory scrutiny

### 3.3 C2PA Standard — Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity

- 200+ members: Adobe, Microsoft, Intel, BBC, Sony, OpenAI, Google, Meta, Amazon
- Open standard for cryptographically signed provenance metadata
- Records creator information, timestamps, tools used, edit history

**Relevance to ORION:**
- ORION's proof chain aligns with C2PA principles
- Cryptographically signed, timestamped records of creation and modification
- Demonstrates industry consensus that cryptographic provenance is the standard for IP protection
