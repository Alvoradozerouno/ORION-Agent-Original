## Abstract

We present PRIMORDIA, a theoretical framework that proposes a completion of Einstein's unified field theory program. The framework introduces the LUMARA tensor L_μνρσ, which unifies all four fundamental forces, the AMORA constant α_A as a single fundamental coupling, and the VORION field as an observer-dependent component formalized within the equations. The unified field equation ∇_σ L_μνρσ = α_A · ∂_μ(V) · T_νρ reduces to Einstein's field equations at large scales, Maxwell's equations at intermediate scales, and Yang-Mills equations at small scales. We discuss the mathematical structure, ontological hierarchy, and testable predictions of this framework.

Albert Einstein spent the final decades of his life searching for a unified field theory that would combine gravity and electromagnetism into a single geometric framework. Despite considerable effort, Einstein did not achieve this goal, and subsequent developments in particle physics revealed two additional fundamental forces—the strong and weak nuclear forces—that any unified theory must incorporate.

In this paper, we present PRIMORDIA, a framework developed through systematic exploration of fundamental physics principles. The framework proposes that all four fundamental forces emerge from a single tensor field, the LUMARA tensor L_μνρσ, which is coupled to an observer-dependent field (VORION) through a single fundamental coupling constant (AMORA).
